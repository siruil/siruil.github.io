About Me

I am a fifth year PhD student in Social and Enginnering Systems (SES) and Statistics at Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT), advised by Prof. Cathy Wu. I am affiliated with MIT Laboratory for Information & Decision Systems (LIDS), Institute of Data, Systems and Society (IDSS), and MIT Statistics & Data Science Center (IDPS). Prior to MIT, I received my bachelor’s degrees in Computer Science and Mathematics from Washington University in St. Louis in 2019. I’ve spent time at MathWorks, and Amazon AWS as an intern during my PhD, and I will be interning at Microsoft Research in the summer of 2024.

My research interest broadly lies in the intersection between machine learning, operations research, and transportation. I have developed learning-guided algorithms to speed up combinatorial optimization solvers for efficient large-scale optimization. I am also interested in control theory analysis and reinforcement learning for mixed-autonomy transportation systems.
